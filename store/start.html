Before You Start

Read this page carefully before using the maker. Having everything ready will make the process smooth and fast.

📱

iPhone / App Store only — for now

Direktunes currently generates apps for iPhone only, distributed via the Apple App Store. Android support is coming soon. You will also need a Mac with Xcode to complete the final submission step.

🎒 What you need to prepare

  • 🎵

    Your songs

    Between 3 and 12 audio files. Each file must be under 20 MB.

    3 – 12 songs Max 20 MB per song MP3 · M4A · AAC · WAV
  • 📸

    Artist / band photo

    Displayed full-screen on the app's Home screen. Square crop works best.

    JPG or PNG · min 800 × 800 px
  • 🎨

    Album artwork

    Shown on the Player and Lyrics screens. Must be square.

    JPG or PNG · min 800 × 800 px · square
  • 📲

    App icon

    The icon that appears on the iPhone home screen. No rounded corners needed — iOS adds them automatically.

    JPG or PNG · min 1024 × 1024 px · square
  • 📝

    Lyrics (optional)

    Plain text lyrics for each song. You can also upload a .lrc file per song to enable real-time line-by-line highlighting as the song plays.

    Plain text or .lrc file
  • 📖

    Album notes & credits

    A short text about the album (recording story, inspiration) and the full production credits.

  • 🍎

    Apple Developer account

    Required to publish on the App Store. If you don't have one yet, enrol at developer.apple.com.

    $99 / year
  • 💻

    A Mac with Xcode

    You need a Mac to open the generated project and submit it to the App Store. Xcode is free — download it from the Mac App Store. Not sure what to do after downloading? Read our step-by-step guide →

    Mac only

⚡ How it works

1

Fill in the wizard

Upload your songs, artwork, lyrics and info across 7 simple steps.

2

Download your Xcode project

We build everything automatically and deliver a ready-to-open .zip file.

3

Open in Xcode on your Mac

Double-click the project, select your Apple Developer team, and press Archive. Full guide here →

4

Publish to the App Store

Upload to App Store Connect, test with TestFlight, then submit for review. Apple usually approves within 1–2 days.

Got everything? Let's go.

The wizard takes about 10 minutes to complete.
