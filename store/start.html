Before You Start

Read this page carefully before using the maker. Having everything ready will make the process smooth and fast.

📱

iOS & Android — both supported

Direktunes generates apps for both iPhone (Apple App Store) and Android (Google Play Store). For iOS you will need a Mac with Xcode to complete the final submission step.

🎒 What you need to prepare

  • 🎵

    Your songs

    Between 3 and 12 audio files. Each file must be under 20 MB.

    3 – 12 songs Max 20 MB per song MP3 · M4A · AAC · WAV
  • 📸

    Artist / band photo

    Displayed full-screen on the app's Home screen. Square crop works best.

    JPG or PNG · min 800 × 800 px
  • 🎨

    Album artwork

    Shown on the Player and Lyrics screens. Must be square.

    JPG or PNG · min 800 × 800 px · square
  • 📲

    App icon

    Used for both iOS and Android. No rounded corners needed — each platform applies its own shape automatically.

    JPG or PNG · min 1024 × 1024 px · square
  • 📝

    Lyrics (optional)

    Plain text lyrics for each song. You can also upload a .lrc file per song to enable real-time line-by-line highlighting as the song plays.

    Plain text or .lrc file
  • 📖

    Album notes & credits

    A short text about the album (recording story, inspiration) and the full production credits.

  • 🍎

    Apple Developer account

    Required to publish on the App Store. If you don't have one yet, enrol at developer.apple.com.

    $99 / year
  • 💻

    A Mac with Xcode (iOS only)

    You need a Mac to open the generated iOS project and submit it to the App Store. Xcode is free — download it from the Mac App Store. Not sure what to do after downloading? Read our iOS guide →

    Mac only
  • 🤖

    Google Play Developer account (Android only)

    Required to publish on Google Play. Register once at play.google.com/console — it's a one-time fee.

    $25 one-time
  • 🖥️

    Android Studio (Android only)

    Free IDE for opening and running the Android project. Works on Mac, Windows and Linux. Download at developer.android.com/studio. Not sure what to do after downloading? Read our Android guide →

    Free · Mac · Windows · Linux

⚡ How it works

🍎 iOS — Apple App Store
1

Fill in the wizard

Upload your songs, artwork, lyrics and info across 7 simple steps.

2

Download your Xcode project

We build everything automatically and deliver a ready-to-open .zip file.

3

Open in Xcode on your Mac

Select your Apple Developer team and press Archive.

4

Publish to the App Store

Test with TestFlight, then submit for review. Apple approves within 1–2 days. Full guide →

🤖 Android — Google Play
1

Fill in the wizard

The same wizard generates both iOS and Android — no extra steps.

2

Download your Android Studio project

We build an Android Studio–ready .zip alongside the iOS project.

3

Open in Android Studio

Works on Mac, Windows or Linux. Gradle syncs automatically. Run on a device or emulator.

4

Publish to Google Play

Generate a signed App Bundle, upload to the Play Console, and submit for review. Full guide →

Got everything? Let's go.

The wizard takes about 10 minutes to complete.
